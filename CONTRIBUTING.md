# Contributing

Thanks for helping keep this list useful. New resources, corrections, and broken-link fixes are all welcome.

## How to Suggest a Resource

Open an issue with the "Suggest a Resource" template, or send a pull request that edits `README.md` directly. For a pull request, add your entry to the most relevant section and follow the format already used there.

## What Belongs Here

This list covers papers, tools, datasets, benchmarks, and standards for building, evaluating, and auditing reliable AI agents. An entry should be:

- Real and verifiable, with a working link to the paper, repository, dataset, or specification.
- On topic for at least one section.
- Described in one short, factual sentence that states what the resource does, not why it is good.

## Entry Format

Papers and datasets go in the section table, one row per entry:

```
| [Title](link) | Venue Year | One-sentence summary. | [[Code]](link) |
```

Tools and standards use the one-line format already in those sections: a bold name, the source link, a short description, and a license or access note.

## Quality Bar

- Cite the primary source. Link the paper or the official repository, not a third-party reposting.
- No promotional language and no self-promotion spam. A maintainer may edit a submitted summary for tone and length.
- Keep one entry per resource. When a paper and its dataset both matter, the dataset may also appear in the Datasets section.
